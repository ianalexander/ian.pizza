A Brief History of Facebook Products

Jun 20 2013

Today Facebook has announced their Video for Instagram feature to much anticipation. The service allows for 15 second videos, stylistic filters, and native integration into the existing Instagram platform.

Time will tell how successful Instagram for Facebook is, but in the meantime we can look back on Facebook’s product history. This is not meant to be a “Facebook sucks” post, there are many quality of pieces of innovation in this list including the Facebook Open Graph Platform, Likes, and Timeline.

This history of products gives us a chance to review product development at Facebook and identify some of the trends that may make a product successful or not.

Observations

From 2006 to 2012, Innovative products at Facebook have greatly outperformed their clone counterparts. Innovative products performed approximately 66% better than cloned products. Likes, Timeline, Open Graph have all been long-lived and successful. Marketplace, Places, and Deals have suffered (current data suggests Poke will have the same fate). From 2006 to 2012, Facebook never launched a clone that has not been discontinued.

But not all innovative products are successful, Beacon and Credits both met an unfortunate end. Data suggests Facebook Home will head to the wayside as well.

As we watch Facebook unveil it’s latest Vine clone, data suggests that this product may struggle to grow as quickly as its counterparts.
